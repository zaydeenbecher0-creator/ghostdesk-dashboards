How It Works

The Gap

Every founder has something to say.
Almost none of them say it.

Not because they lack opinions. Because writing a good post takes longer than closing the meeting it's supposed to promote.

01

The calendar always wins

Board decks, term sheets, and portfolio calls outrank a LinkedIn post every single time. So the post never gets written — not once, not ever.

02

Silence reads as absence

The partners and founders who post consistently are the ones LPs and co-investors remember when the next round comes together. Everyone else is invisible by default.

03

Ghostwriters sound like ghostwriters

Generic agencies produce generic LinkedIn voice — the same three hooks, the same fake vulnerability. It reads as content, not as you.

Questions

Before you book.

01Do you write in my voice, or yours?+

Yours. Every post is built from your own voice notes — your phrasing, your opinions, your cadence. We edit and structure; we don't invent a persona.

02What if I miss a week's recording?+

We work from a backlog of your prior notes and past posts, so the calendar never goes empty. You just record again when you're ready.

03Do you post on my behalf?+

By default, no — we schedule drafts for your review and you approve before anything goes live. Full autonomous posting is available on request.

04Can I cancel anytime?+

Yes. Every desk is month to month with 30 days' notice — no long-term contract.
